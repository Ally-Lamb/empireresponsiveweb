How to join Empire Seniors

Uniform

Empire recently have changed their uniforms to straight black jackets with gold trimmings and piping. They wear the Shimmer tights from company Dancewear as well as their black belts and iconic Crown brand on each upper sleeve. The hats were reused from previous years however a new red feather is now displayed running up the hat and being the only team in NZ to commit to black boots has seen this team stand out like no other.

Who are Empire

Empire are a Senior Grade marching team based in Canterbury. Coaches Keryn Cawood and Sam O'Connell train their team of 15 girls twice a week to prepare them for the local competitions held here at Nunweek Park each Saturday as well as work towards the main championships - South Island Championships and the Marching NZ National Championships. The team originated 6 years ago and have been building up their strength each season making them a force to be reckoned with.

Sponsors

The Redwood Tavern Christchurch have generously been financially supporting Empire for the 2021/2022 season. Whilst hosting the team with their great customer service, food and drinks after each competition to run through the judges sheets they have also heald our functions and fundraising events. Empire would like to say a huge thank you to The Redwood Tavern for all its ongoing support and sponsorship with our amazing new Shell jackets.

Empire take out Canterbury Championships 2022!

With the confirmation that the Marching New Zealand Championships had been cancelled due to Covid 19's Omicron variant, Empire held a team meeting at the start of February to see whether the team wished to continue competing the second half of the season.

As the teams commitment to the season had not wavered, entry into the Canterbury Championships commenced and the Team proceeded with their trainings under the Sports NZ guidelines. Competing against other Canterbury Senior teams where the closeness in points had teams alternating on titles week to week, Empire took out the title of winning 1st Technical Drill team and 1st overall Canterbury Champion Senior Team, a first time for the team who have been strongly competing in the Canterbury association for 6 years.
